Hero & market tiles

  • India · US · Gold · Crypto tiles mirror the sheet's Summary. Tapping one refocuses the whole screen — chart, readout and label follow. Tap again for total.
  • The eye masks every number (blur, not removal — layout never shifts). Same pattern as the Pay tab balance pill.
  • Day change is close-over-close from the synced daily bars; the sync chip shows staleness instead of a spinner.

Performance chart

  • One y-axis, ₹ only. vs index ⇄ overlays two references: the gray line is the same cashflows bought into Nifty 50 / S&P 500 on the same dates, the dotted line is money invested. The gap between emerald and gray is your alpha, visible per range.
  • Scrub anywhere — the readout above the chart becomes the scrubbed date; release to return to "now". No tooltips floating over data.
  • Ranges are windows over locally stored daily bars — switching is instant and offline.

Allocation (the Google-Finance upgrade)

  • A drillable treemap instead of a donut: area = weight, so a 26% Gold position is felt, not read from a legend. Market → sector → holding, breadcrumb to climb back.
  • Tiles keep their market's hue; the list below always mirrors the current level with exact ₹ and %.
  • Donuts hide small slices and force color-matching across a legend — the treemap direct-labels everything ≥ ~5% and groups the tail into "Other".

Default is ±4%/day per holding. A quiet threshold check runs after each price sync — alerts fire from data already on the device, nothing is polled server-side.

Holding detail

  • Price history with your buys marked on the line — cost-versus-price is instantly visible. Sells get hollow markers.
  • Stat grid: units, invested, value, day move, XIRR of this position, and what the same cashflows in its market index returned.
  • Trade list is the receipt-feed pattern from the Pay tab — same rhythm, same date grouping.

Alert engine

  • Rule = |close ÷ prev-close − 1| ≥ threshold per holding, evaluated after the daily sync and on any manual refresh. Default ±4%, per-item override (crypto wants ±8%, index funds ±2.5%).
  • Notification says what moved, how much, and the position size — enough to decide whether to open the app.
  • Deliberately no schedules: your ask was flags on significant movement only. The 90-day backtest on your real holdings: — alerts at ±4% — roughly one every other day; at ±6% it's ~2/week.

Data notes (what the numbers are)

Validation. India current value matches the sheet's INR rollup to a rounding error, and gold invested matches the Gold tab to the rupee — the trade-derived model reproduces the spreadsheet. Gold value uses international spot (GC=F × USDINR ÷ 31.1035) — a few percent under Indian retail rates, which carry import duty; a premium factor will be a setting. US is valued at the latest Yahoo close, so it reads slightly above the sheet's static snapshot. XIRR is bisection-solved on actual dated cashflows; the benchmark comparison buys index units with each cashflow on its date — a true "what if the same money went into the index".
